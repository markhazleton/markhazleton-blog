The Creation of ShareSmallBiz.com

A Platform for Small Business Success

The idea for ShareSmallBiz.com was born from the successful partnership between Jonathan Roper and Mark Hazleton in building WichitaSewer.com, a digital presence for Jonathan’s sewer and drain service company. This experience highlighted the power of collaboration, strategic digital marketing, and the importance of small businesses supporting each other to thrive in an increasingly competitive marketplace.

Recognizing the Need for a Shared Platform

// Bootstrap Icon for collaboration After launching WichitaSewer.com, Jonathan and Mark saw firsthand how a well-executed online strategy could transform a business. However, they also recognized a gap: many small businesses struggle to gain visibility, attract customers, and compete against larger corporations.

They asked themselves:

  • What if small business owners worked together to amplify their marketing efforts?
  • What if they shared knowledge, cross-promoted each other, and pooled resources to reach a larger audience?
  • Could a dedicated platform be the solution to breaking through the barriers that small businesses face?

From these questions, ShareSmallBiz.com was born—a community-driven social media platform designed to connect, empower, and grow small businesses through collaboration and shared marketing.

The State of Small Business Marketing

In today's competitive business landscape, small businesses face unique challenges that require innovative solutions and robust support systems. ShareSmallBiz.com steps in as a catalyst, providing a platform that encourages small businesses to not only exist but thrive through collaboration and shared resources.

As the backbone of our economies, small businesses contribute significantly to community vitality and economic diversity. ShareSmallBiz.com harnesses this power by fostering an environment where businesses can collaborate on projects, share insights, and overcome challenges collectively.

This online community is more than just a networking site; it's a launchpad for sustainable growth, enabling businesses to expand their reach and impact through strategic partnerships and shared marketing efforts. Building ShareSmallBiz.com

With the vision in place, Jonathan and Mark set out to create a platform where business owners could network, exchange ideas, and cross-promote each other’s businesses. Leveraging Mark’s experience in web development and Jonathan’s entrepreneurial insights, they structured the site around key goals:

Collaboration Over Competition

  • ShareSmallBiz.com encourages businesses to support one another, instead of competing in isolation.
  • Business owners can cross-promote services, pooling their audiences for greater exposure.

Expanding Reach Without Paid Ads

  • Instead of relying solely on expensive paid advertising, small businesses can leverage organic growth by sharing each other's content.
  • By engaging in shared marketing efforts, each business increases its audience reach exponentially.

Creating a Network of Trusted Entrepreneurs

  • A place where small business owners can ask questions, share experiences, and find solutions together.
  • Members can partner on projects, refer clients, and build strategic relationships.

A Marketplace for Small Businesses (Future Expansion)

  • The platform is designed to showcase products and services, allowing members to discover and support each other’s businesses.
  • A potential drop-shipping model and service-based marketplace are planned for future releases.

From Idea to Reality

The platform is currently in its beta phase, with a growing community of early adopters. Entrepreneurs are sharing ideas, promoting each other’s businesses, and collaborating in ways that weren’t previously possible. The project has already begun attracting investment, with opportunities for founders and backers to be part of the movement.

A Movement for Small Business Success

Mark and Jonathan firmly believe that small businesses are the lifeblood of the economy. By creating ShareSmallBiz.com, they aim to redefine how small businesses interact, support, and grow together—proving that when business owners work together, everyone wins.

The journey from WichitaSewer.com to ShareSmallBiz.com is just beginning, but the potential for a new wave of community-driven business growth is undeniable.
